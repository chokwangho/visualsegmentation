Scott R Klemmer

Assistant Professor of Computer Science

Enlightened trial and error - prototyping - is the pivotal activity that structures design innovation, collaboration, and creativity. My group's research seeks to enable a broader community of users to design interactive systems, and to enable expert designers to iterate more quickly and effectively. To accomplish this, we have introduced - and are actively researching - techniques for users to demonstrate interactive behavior, sample existing design elements to create new ones, and more tightly integrate the creation and evaluation aspects of design. We explore these issues on mobile, desktop, and web platforms; and are particularly excited about designing pervasive interactions that integrate our physical and digital environments. (full bio)

About

The Stanford HCI Group works across disciplines to understand the intersection between humans and computers. Our research has explored ubiquitous computing, alternative methods of input, tools for enhancing designers' ability to create and explore their ideas, and rapid prototyping tools for the Web and physical devices. The HCI Group is associated with the Stanford Graphics Lab.

What's New

The October issue of the Stanford Report has an article about field work at Jasper Ridge, including HCI PhD Ron Yeh's work on ButterflyNet. Bjorn Hartmann's paper Design As Exploration: Creating Interface Alternatives through Parallel Authoring and Runtime Tuning has been awarded Best Student Paper at UIST 2008. The Stanford design school's Journal of Design, Ambidextrous, has just completed its latest issue, themed "Getting it on."

Getting Involved

For Masters students, the HCI track is a popular concentration, and involves classes in interaction design and human-computer interaction. We also have several PhD students working in our group. Our PhD students' projects range from ubiquitous computing technologies that meld physical & digital devices, to tools for programmers & designers, and beyond. For both Masters and PhD students, admission is done through the Computer Science department.
